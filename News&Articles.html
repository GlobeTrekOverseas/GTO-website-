Step-by-Step Guide: How to Apply for a Student Visa to the UK (2025 Edition)

Everything you need to know about the UK student visa process for 2025

Published: 28 August, 2025 Reading Time: 10 minutes
  • Home
  • News & Articles
  • UK Student Visa Guide

The United Kingdom continues to be one of the most popular destinations for international students, thanks to its world-class universities, globally recognized degrees, and career opportunities.

Before beginning your journey, you need to secure a UK student visa. This guide breaks down the UK student visa process 2025 into simple, step-by-step instructions.

1

Check Your Eligibility

You must meet the following requirements:

  • Hold an unconditional offer from a licensed UK university.
  • Receive a CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies) from your institution.
  • Show proof of sufficient financial funds.
  • Meet English language requirements (IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, or equivalent).
2

Prepare Your Documents

Here are the key documents you will need:

Document Purpose Notes
Valid Passport Proof of identity Must be valid for entire duration of stay
CAS Letter Issued by your university Essential for visa application
Proof of Funds Bank statements, loan, or sponsor letter Must cover tuition + living expenses
English Test Results Language proficiency IELTS/TOEFL/PTE accepted
Academic Records Certificates & transcripts As per course requirements
TB Test Report Health requirement Mandatory for Indian applicants

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1: How much money do I need for a UK student visa?

You must show funds for tuition (first year) + living expenses:

  • Â£1,334/month in London
  • Â£1,023/month outside London (up to 9 months).
Q2: Can I work while studying?

Yes, up to 20 hours per week during term-time and full-time during breaks.

Q3: How long can I stay after my studies?

With the Graduate Route Visa, you can stay:

  • 2 years (Bachelor's/Master's)
  • 3 years (PhD).
Q4: What if my visa is refused?

You may appeal or reapply after addressing the reason for refusal.

Q5: Can I bring my family with me?

Yes, for certain postgraduate courses (9+ months) or government-sponsored studies.
